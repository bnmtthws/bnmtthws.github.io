This one was a long time coming - we're thrilled to finally share Aleks Anoshina's behavioural insights into mechanosensory control of egg-laying behaviour in Aedes aegypti. [Read the pre-print here](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.03.08.710377v1)

Aleks, an undergraduate Honours student, showed alongside fellow undergrads Lauren Semkow and Annie Zeng, that Aedes aegypti egg-laying behaviour is deeply influenced by surface texture - rough beats smooth, every time. Former technician Nick Tochor filmed this behaviour in action and showed that gravid mosquitoes can't wait to get off a smooth surface and onto a rough patch, where they will preferentially deposit their eggs. This work sets the stage for a major current focus of the lab on the molecular, cellular, and behavioural roles of mechansensation in guiding mosquito behaviour.
