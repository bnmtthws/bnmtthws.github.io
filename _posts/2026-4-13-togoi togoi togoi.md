Happy genome day to the coastal rock pool mosquito *Aedes togoi*!

After a long journey of sequencing, assembling, scaffolding, curating, polishing, annotating, and much more, Jonathan Chiang has completed a chromosome-scale genome for our  favourite salt-tolerant coastal mosquito. Read the pre-print here: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.04.09.717544v1

Along with dream team Andrea Durant and her Ph.D. student Elissa Khodikian (UW Biology), Jonathan, along with former lab members Dan Peach, Orna Phelan and Ana Parra, worked hard to get this assembly together alongside transcriptomic insights into its unique larval 'salt gland.' This genome opens the door to new questions about osmoregulation, sensory systems, and ecological specialization in mosquitoes. Huge thanks to everyone involved in the project!
